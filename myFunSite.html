Air Jordan 3 "Free Throw Line" (2018)

In 1987, Michael Jordan had stunted one of the most memorable actins in basketball history. During his iconic dunk, Jordan was wearing the third set of the Jordan collection, the Air Jordan 3. Nike and Jordan had decided to bring back the shoe and release replicas of the sneakers in 2018 with the added red line across the sole of the shoe to commemorate the event.

Air Jordan 1 "Dior" (2020)

It goes without saying that the price of sneakers has significantly increased over the years. With the introduction of new sneaker design and collaborations with other designers and artists, the 21st century has seen one of the highest jumps in prices and demand. In 2020, Jordan had announced a collab with Dior fashion in designing the infamous Air Jordan 1. What started as a rumor eventually became a reality and once the shoes hit the market they origianlly sold for $2,000. As demands for these designer sneakers quickly increased, many have begun reselling the sneakers at a base price of $10,000 making it the most expensive sneakers in the game.
